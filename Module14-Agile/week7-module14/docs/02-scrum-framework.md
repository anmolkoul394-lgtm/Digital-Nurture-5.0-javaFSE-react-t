# 2. Scrum Framework — Roles, Ceremonies & Artifacts

Scrum is the most widely used Agile framework, organizing work into
fixed-length iterations called **Sprints** (usually 1–4 weeks).

## Scrum Roles
| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Product Owner** | Owns the Product Backlog, decides WHAT gets built and in what priority order, represents the customer/business |
| **Scrum Master** | Facilitates the process, removes blockers, coaches the team on Scrum practices — not a project manager who assigns tasks |
| **Development Team** | Cross-functional group that actually builds the increment; self-organizing on HOW to do the work |

## Scrum Ceremonies (Events)
| Ceremony | When | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Sprint Planning** | Start of sprint | Team selects backlog items and plans how to deliver them |
| **Daily Scrum (Standup)** | Every day, ~15 min | What did I do yesterday, what will I do today, any blockers |
| **Sprint Review** | End of sprint | Demo the working increment to stakeholders, gather feedback |
| **Sprint Retrospective** | End of sprint, after review | Team reflects: what went well, what didn't, what to improve |

## Scrum Artifacts
| Artifact | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Product Backlog** | Ordered list of everything that might be needed in the product — owned/prioritized by the Product Owner |
| **Sprint Backlog** | The subset of Product Backlog items selected for the current sprint, plus a plan to deliver them |
| **Increment** | The sum of all completed Product Backlog items during a sprint — must be in a usable, "Done" state |

## Definition of Done (DoD)
A shared checklist the team agrees on that defines when a story is
actually complete — e.g. "code written, unit tests passing, code reviewed,
merged to main, deployed to staging, acceptance criteria verified." Without
a DoD, "done" means different things to different people.
