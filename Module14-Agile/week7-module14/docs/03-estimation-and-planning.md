# 3. Agile Estimation & Planning Techniques

## Story Points
A relative, unitless measure of the effort/complexity/uncertainty of a
user story — NOT a direct measure of hours. Teams typically use a
Fibonacci-like scale: **1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21** (bigger gaps at higher
numbers reflect growing uncertainty in large items).

## Planning Poker
A consensus-based estimation technique:
1. The story is read aloud/discussed.
2. Each team member privately picks a card (story point value) that
   represents their estimate.
3. Everyone reveals their card at the same time (avoids anchoring bias).
4. If estimates differ significantly, the high/low estimators explain
   their reasoning, then the team re-votes.
5. Repeat until consensus (or close enough) is reached.

## Sprint Planning Process
1. Product Owner presents the highest-priority backlog items.
2. Team asks clarifying questions, estimates/re-estimates if needed.
3. Team pulls in items until they hit their **capacity** (based on past
   velocity), and defines a Sprint Goal.
4. Selected items + a delivery plan = the Sprint Backlog.

## Velocity
The average number of story points a team completes per sprint,
calculated from past sprints. Used to forecast how much work fits in
future sprints — NOT to compare teams against each other (every team's
points mean something different).

## Burndown Charts
A chart showing remaining work (story points or hours) over the course of
a sprint. The ideal line goes straight from total-work to zero by sprint
end; the actual line shows real progress — a flat or rising actual line
signals the team is falling behind or scope is creeping in.
